Fear of immigration enforcement keeps many community members from seeking healthcare, even when they're seriously ill. Immigrant-friendly clinics actively work to reduce these barriers and serve all community members.

## What Makes a Clinic Immigrant-Friendly

- **No status questions**: Don't ask about immigration status or require ID
- **Language access**: Interpretation available in relevant languages
- **Privacy policies**: Clear policies limiting information sharing with ICE
- **Sliding scale fees**: Affordable care for those without insurance
- **Cultural competence**: Staff trained in serving diverse communities

## Why This Gives Hope

When healthcare is accessible and safe, families are healthier and more able to thrive. Some clinics have become models of how to provide welcoming care that reaches everyone.

## Current Resources

Several federally qualified health centers in Baltimore serve patients regardless of status. Community health workers can help connect families with these resources.

## Strengthening This Work

Advocacy continues to push for stronger patient privacy protections and increased funding for safety-net clinics serving immigrant communities.
