Language barriers can prevent immigrants from accessing critical services. If you speak a language other than English, your skills can make a real difference in someone's life.

## Where Interpreters Are Needed

- **Legal settings**: Attorney consultations, court preparation
- **Medical appointments**: Doctor visits, hospital stays
- **Schools**: Parent-teacher conferences, IEP meetings
- **Government offices**: Benefits applications, ID renewals
- **Community events**: Know Your Rights trainings, workshops

## Skills Required

- Fluency in English and at least one other language
- Ability to interpret accurately without adding opinions
- Cultural competency and sensitivity
- Confidentiality and professionalism

## Getting Started

Many organizations offer interpreter training:
- Basic interpretation techniques
- Legal and medical terminology
- Ethics and boundaries
- Trauma-informed approaches

## Time Commitment

Varies by organization—from occasional appointments to regular weekly shifts.

## Why This Matters

Access to language support is a civil right, but services often fall short. Community interpreters fill crucial gaps and help ensure everyone can advocate for themselves.
