Citizenship workshops help eligible immigrants navigate the naturalization process. Volunteers don't need legal expertise—you'll be trained to assist with specific tasks under attorney supervision.

## Workshop Roles

- **Greeters**: Welcome applicants and manage flow
- **Screeners**: Help determine eligibility using a checklist
- **Application assistants**: Help complete N-400 forms
- **Reviewers**: Check applications for completeness
- **Interpreters**: Assist non-English speakers

## Training Provided

Before each workshop, volunteers receive:
- Overview of the naturalization process
- Training on specific volunteer role
- Information about common issues
- Guidance on what questions to escalate

## Typical Workshop Day

- Arrive early for setup and volunteer briefing
- Work directly with applicants (typically 2-4 hours)
- Help with cleanup and follow-up tasks

## Why This Matters

Becoming a citizen provides security and opens doors—but the process is complex and expensive. Free workshops dramatically increase access, especially for those who can't afford private attorneys.
