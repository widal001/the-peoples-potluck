Social media can be a powerful tool for shifting narratives and mobilizing support. You don't need a huge following to make a difference—consistent, thoughtful engagement matters.

## Effective Social Media Advocacy

### Share, Don't Just Like

- Repost content from immigrant-led organizations
- Add your own perspective when sharing
- Include calls to action

### Counter Misinformation

- Respond to false claims with facts and sources
- Report hate speech and harassment
- Avoid amplifying harmful content by quote-tweeting

### Uplift Immigrant Voices

- Center stories told by immigrants themselves
- Avoid "savior" framing
- Credit and tag original sources

## Best Practices

- **Be consistent**: Regular posting is more effective than occasional bursts
- **Be accurate**: Verify information before sharing
- **Be strategic**: Coordinate with organizations on timing
- **Be careful**: Protect identifying information

## Organizations to Follow

Follow local and national immigrant rights organizations to stay informed and know when to amplify urgent messages.

## Why This Matters

Public opinion shapes policy. When more people share accurate, humanizing content about immigration, it shifts the broader conversation and creates political space for change.
