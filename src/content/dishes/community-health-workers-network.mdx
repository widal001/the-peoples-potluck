Community health workers—often called promotoras in Latino communities—serve as bridges between healthcare systems and immigrant communities. They provide education, navigation, and support in languages and cultural contexts that make sense for their communities.

## What Promotoras Do

- **Health education**: Teaching about preventive care, nutrition, and managing chronic conditions
- **System navigation**: Helping community members access healthcare regardless of insurance status
- **Language access**: Providing interpretation and ensuring culturally-appropriate care
- **Trust building**: Creating connections between hesitant community members and health services

## Why This Matters

Fear of immigration enforcement keeps many community members from seeking healthcare. Trusted community health workers help bridge this gap, ensuring that fear doesn't prevent people from getting the care they need.
